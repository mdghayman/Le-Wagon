Introduction¶

Question:¶

Should Olist remove underperforming sellers from its marketplace?

Answer:¶

Yes, under certain conditions, and dependent on expert knowledge.

Approach¶

  • Examines Olist database, to determine which factors may isolate sales and factors reducing profitability.
  • Greatest opportunities lie in list of sellers, with very different circumstances.
  • Incoporating consequences of star ratings, highlight least profitable sellers.
  • Consider the potential reduced IT costs, due to reduced order numbers.

Underperforming sellers: Other considerations¶

I recommend ending sales from those sellers that are underperforming to the greatest extent, with this model taking the cost of negative reviews into account.

If customers are limited to ordering from sellers located close to them, this may reduce delivery times and bad reviews. This can be tested, to some extent, by modelling the distance of customer to seller against review scores, and/or delivery time versus expected delivery time.

Scaling IT costs¶

Costs of Olist's IT infrastructure are related to total orders. These costs also affect profitability.

Scaling IT costs: Context¶

Olist's IT costs increase with the total (i.e. global) amount of orders processed. However, economies of scale mean that the average cost per total orders decreases as total orders increase further.

This relationship is roughly estimated by a the following equation, to determine the constant A:

A = $\frac{Total IT costs}{\sqrt{orders}}$

Where ITcost is the total cost of IT to Olist, and Orders of is the value of total orders.

Since the total IT costs since the birth of the marketplace have amounted to 500,000 BRL, the relationship can be specified as follows:

Scaling IT costs: Other considerations¶

While our modelling is useful, the relationship between orders and IT costs is probably more complex.

At different scales, the nature of this relationship is likely to change (e.g. shifting more of storage and computing power to cloud computing, and more cost effective enterprise-level computing).
